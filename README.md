## 🌐 About the Project

**GyaanSetu** is a next-generation, full-stack EdTech platform built to solve two of India's most critical and deeply interconnected education crises — the **practical skills gap** and the **regional language barrier**.

India produces over 1.5 million engineering graduates every year. Yet, a staggering majority of them are not industry-ready. Companies routinely report that fresh hires require 6–12 months of internal training before they can contribute meaningfully. Simultaneously, millions of genuinely talented students from Tier 2, Tier 3 cities, and rural India remain cut off from the world's best online education — not because they lack intelligence or ambition, but simply because that education is delivered exclusively in English.

GyaanSetu is the answer to both. It is a single, unified platform that offers:

- **Industry-simulated, hands-on coding labs** — so students learn by doing, not just by reading.
- **Regional language course delivery** — starting with Gujarati and Hindi, so no student is excluded because of the language they grew up speaking.

The name itself carries the mission: *Gyaan* (Knowledge) + *Setu* (Bridge) — GyaanSetu is literally the bridge between where Indian students are, and where they need to be.

## 🔴 Problem Statement

### Problem 1 — The Practical Skills Gap in Fresh Graduates

Despite strong academic records, most fresh engineering and computer science graduates struggle to meet industry expectations. Colleges focus heavily on theoretical instruction — lectures, textbooks, and exam-based evaluation — while offering little to no exposure to real-world coding environments, debugging practice, or project-based learning.

#### 📌 Core Issue
Companies hiring fresh graduates consistently find a disconnect: candidates may demonstrate strong theoretical knowledge and impressive academic scores, yet struggle to solve basic programming problems, debug code, or apply learned concepts in real-world contexts.

#### 🔍 Root Cause
College curricula are predominantly lecture-driven and exam-oriented, with minimal focus on industry-simulated labs or project-based learning. Students complete degrees without ever building, testing, or deploying code in environments that mirror actual workplace conditions.

#### ❌ Specific Pain Points:
| Pain Point | Impact |
|---|---|
| Curricula prioritise memorisation over application | Students can recall concepts but cannot apply them |
| No industry-simulation labs or live project assignments | Zero hands-on exposure during 4 years of college |
| Students cannot debug, deploy, or collaborate on real codebases | Unfit for day-one productivity at a job |
| Companies face high training costs and delayed productivity | Economic loss for industry; delayed career growth for students |
| No live debugging and problem-solving practice environments | Students have never faced a real error in a real project |
| Significant gap between academic evaluation and industry requirements | Marks ≠ Skill. GPAs don't reflect employability. |

---

### Problem 2 — The Regional Language Barrier in Global EdTech

Talented students from non-metro and rural areas of India are effectively excluded from elite global education. Platforms like Coursera, edX, and MIT OpenCourseWare deliver content exclusively in English, creating a significant accessibility gap for learners whose first language is Hindi, Gujarati, Tamil, Bengali, or any other regional Indian language.

#### 📌 Core Issue
Exceptionally talented students from non-metro areas of India — who are highly capable in their respective technical fields — are effectively excluded from the world's most prestigious online courses, certification programs, and educational content simply because these resources are only available in English.

#### 🔍 Root Cause
Global EdTech platforms and elite institutions have not invested in localizing their content into regional Indian languages. The assumption that high-quality education is accessible to anyone with internet access overlooks a fundamental language barrier that prevents millions from benefiting.

#### ❌ Specific Pain Points:
| Pain Point | Impact |
|---|---|
| Top-tier online courses and certifications are English-only | Regional language learners are locked out entirely |
| No high-quality localisation into regional Indian languages exists | No alternative pathway for non-English learners |
| Language barriers prevent skilled learners from reaching their potential | Talent is wasted due to language, not capability |
| Geographic and linguistic inequality widens the urban-rural education divide | Tier 1 city students thrive; Tier 2/3 students fall further behind |
| Students with domain expertise but limited English fluency are left behind | A student who understands data structures in Gujarati is treated as a beginner in English |

### Unified Problem Statement

> *India's education ecosystem faces a dual crisis: fresh graduates enter the workforce with strong theoretical knowledge but lack the hands-on coding and problem-solving skills that industry demands, while simultaneously, highly capable students from non-metro regions are denied access to world-class learning resources due to the English-only nature of global EdTech platforms.*
>
> *Both gaps stem from structural failures — curricula that never moved beyond lectures, and an international ed-tech industry that never looked beyond English. The result is a talent pool that is academically sound but practically under-equipped, and a vast segment of India's youth locked out of quality upskilling altogether.*

### Step-by-Step Workflow:

**Step 1 — Discovery**
Student lands on GyaanSetu, reads about the platform, sees featured courses in their language, and decides to sign up.

**Step 2 — Onboarding**
Student creates an account, selects their preferred learning language (e.g., Gujarati), and sets a learning goal (e.g., "Get a job in Web Development").

**Step 3 — Course Enrollment**
Student browses the Course Catalog filtered by language and difficulty, views the Course Detail page, and enrolls (free or paid).

**Step 4 — Active Learning**
Student watches lessons in their chosen language with regional subtitles. After each concept, a lab is unlocked.

**Step 5 — Hands-On Lab**
Student enters the Lab Environment, reads instructions in Gujarati, writes code in the browser editor, runs it, debugs it, and submits for auto-evaluation.

**Step 6 — Project Work**
Student takes on an industry-simulation project, tracks milestones on the Kanban board, collaborates if team-based, and submits via GitHub link or file upload.

**Step 7 — Certification**
Upon completing a course (all lessons + labs + final project), student earns a certificate, gains XP, and rises on the leaderboard.

**Step 8 — Continuous Growth**
Student returns daily (streaks!), completes more labs, earns badges, and builds a portfolio visible on their public profile.

```
Dashboard
```

```
Dashboard → Labs Section
  → Lab Catalog (filter by difficulty)
  → Lab Card (preview modal)
  → Lab Environment Page (3-panel view)
  → [Write Code → Run → Debug → Fix → Run again cycle]
  → "Submit Lab" button
  → Auto-evaluation Results Screen (score, pass/fail per test case)

```

### Flow 5 — Project Workflow
```
Dashboard → Projects
  → Project Catalog (filter by industry / tech stack)
  → Project Detail
  → Project Workspace (Kanban + Brief + Team)
  → Build milestones
  → Submit via GitHub link or file upload
  → Instructor Feedback received
  → Certificate / Badge awarded
```

### Flow 6 — Profile & Achievements
```
Dashboard → Profile (via sidebar or avatar)
  → Student Profile Page (public view)
  → Switch to "Achievements" tab
  → View earned badges + locked badges
  → Click "View Leaderboard"
  → Leaderboard Page (Weekly / Monthly / All Time)
```

### Flow 7 — Language Switch Mid-Session
```
Any authenticated page
  → Click Language Toggle (EN | हिंदी | ગુજ) in Navbar
  → Language Dropdown appears with 6 language options
  → Select new language
  → Page re-renders all content in selected language
  → Toast: "Language changed to ગુજરાતી ✓"
  → Preference saved automatically to profile
```

## 🌍 Social Impact

GyaanSetu is not just a product — it is a mission.

### 📊 The Scale of the Problem We're Solving:
- **1.5 million+** engineering graduates produced in India every year
- **~45%** considered unemployable by industry standards (NASSCOM estimates)
- **600 million+** people in India speak Hindi as a primary language
- **55 million+** people speak Gujarati as a primary language
- **0** major EdTech platforms offer full-depth course content in Gujarati
- **< 5** platforms offer any meaningful Hindi content beyond basic subtitles

### 🎯 GyaanSetu's Impact Targets:
| Metric | Target (Year 1) | Target (Year 3) |
|---|---|---|
| Students Enrolled | 10,000 | 250,000 |
| Labs Completed | 50,000 | 1,000,000 |
| Regional Language Courses | 140 | 500+ |
| Languages Supported | 3 | 6+ |
| Placement-Ready Graduates | 1,000 | 50,000 |
| Tier 2/3 City Students Reached | 60% of user base | 70% of user base |

### 🤲 Who Benefits:
- **Rural and semi-urban students** — who could never access Coursera or edX due to language
- **First-generation learners** — who are the first in their family to enter tech
- **Women in tech** — from conservative backgrounds where English was a barrier
- **Industry** — receives genuinely work-ready engineers, reducing training costs
- **India's GDP** — a more skilled workforce drives economic growth
